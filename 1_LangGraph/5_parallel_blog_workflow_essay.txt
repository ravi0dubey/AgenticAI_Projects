1. Language

IRCC verifies that you meet the language requirement (English or French), which applies to applicants aged 18–54 at the time of application.
They check:

Proof of English/French proficiency (e.g., test results, education in English/French, etc.)

Interview/test performance if applicable.

2. Prohibitions

IRCC confirms you are not prohibited from becoming a citizen, meaning:

You have no criminal charges or convictions (in or outside Canada) that make you ineligible.

You’re not under a removal order.

You’re not serving a sentence, probation, or parole.

You haven’t been involved in war crimes, terrorism, etc.

3. Physical Presence

IRCC checks that you’ve lived in Canada for at least:

1,095 days (3 years) out of the 5 years before your application date.
They use your travel history, CBSA records, and other documents to confirm this.
